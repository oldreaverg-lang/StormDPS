🌀 How to Use This App

This tool visualizes the true destructive potential of hurricanes — going beyond the simple Category 1-5 scale to show how much total wind energy a storm carries and the damage it can cause.

Key Metrics

  • Damage Potential (DPS) — A 0-100 score combining storm surge, wind field size, wind speed, and forward speed. A large Cat 1 like Sandy can score as high as a compact Cat 5 — reflecting equal real-world danger.
  • Wind Energy (IKE) — Total kinetic energy of all winds in Terajoules (TJ). Captures both wind speed AND storm size in one number.
  • Storm Surge — Estimated open-water surge potential in feet, driven by pressure and wind.

Why Not Just Use Categories?

The Saffir-Simpson scale only measures peak wind speed at the center. It ignores the storm's size, which is often the biggest factor in damage. Hurricane Ike (2008) made landfall as "only" a Cat 2, but its enormous wind field caused $40 billion in damage across the entire Texas coast.

Quick Reference

  • TJ (Terajoules) — A unit of energy. 1 TJ = 1 trillion joules.
  • mph — Miles per hour (wind speed).
  • mb / hPa — Millibars, a pressure unit. Lower = stronger storm. Normal sea-level is ~1013 mb.
  • kt — Knots (1 kt = 1.15 mph). Used for storm movement speed.

Getting Started

Pick any storm from Historic Storms in the sidebar, or type a name like "Harvey" and its year. Use the timeline slider to step through the storm's life cycle and watch the wind field change on the map.
